SkillGuard — Scoring Logic Specification (V1)

1. Core Principles
- Explainability First: Every score must trace back to specific evidence (e.g., Found 'Optimized' near 'Node.js').
- Highest Signal Wins: If a skill is found multiple times, we retain the strongest classification (e.g., if a skill is both "Mentioned" and "Strong", it is classified as "Strong").
- Pattern Over Quantity: We measure distinct concepts for DSA, not raw keyword repetition.

----------------------------------------------------------------

2. Skill Classification Heuristics (The "Brain")
We classify every skill found in the resume into one of three levels using Regex Context Analysis.

Level: MENTIONED (1 pt)
Definition: Found in text, but no context.
Detection Logic: 
- Exact case-insensitive match.
- Default state if no other context found.

Level: USED (2 pts)
Definition: Practical application in projects.
Detection Logic: 
- Location: Found inside the "Projects" section.
- Context: Found within 10 words of "Weak Verbs": used, built, implemented, developed, worked on.

Level: STRONG (3 pts)
Definition: Ownership, optimization, or leadership.
Detection Logic: 
- Context: Found within 10 words of "Strong Verbs": architected, optimized, scaled, deployed, led, designed, migrated.
- Frequency: Mentioned > 3 times across document (implies core competency).

Conflict Resolution Rule:
Final_Skill_Level = MAX(Existing_Level, New_Detected_Level)

----------------------------------------------------------------

3. Weighted Scoring Formula (The Math)
The Final Score (0–100) is a weighted sum of four independent dimensions.

Formula:
FinalScore = (RoleFit * 0.40) + (ProjectDepth * 0.25) + (Experience * 0.20) + (Fundamentals * 0.15)

A. Role Fit (Weight: 40%)
- Goal: Does the candidate match the job description?
- Source: src/data/roles.json (Static definition of required skills per role).
- Logic:
  Match Count = (Candidate Skills INTERSECT Required Role Skills).
  Score = (Match Count / Total Required Skills) * 40.

B. Project Depth (Weight: 25%)
- Goal: Do they have "hands-on" evidence?
- Logic:
  +5 pts for every STRONG skill found in the Projects section.
  +2 pts for every USED skill found in the Projects section.
- Constraint: Score capped at 25 (prevents keyword stuffing).

C. Experience Relevance (Weight: 20%)
- Goal: Are they a professional match?
- Logic:
  +10 pts for Job Title Match (e.g., "Backend Engineer", "SDE").
  +5 pts per Year of Experience (parsed from dates).
- Constraint: Score capped at 20.

D. Fundamentals / DSA (Weight: 15%)
- Goal: Do they understand CS theory?
- Logic:
  Scan for Distinct Categories (e.g., "Dynamic Programming", "Graph", "Time Complexity").
  +3 pts per distinct category found.
- Anti-Spam Rule: Mentioning "Graph" 5 times counts as 1 category.
- Constraint: Score capped at 15.

----------------------------------------------------------------

4. Decision Thresholds (The Output)
We map the numeric score to a strict hiring recommendation.

Range: 80 – 100
Recommendation: DIRECT_INTERVIEW
Logic: Strong role match + Evidence of "Strong" usage. High confidence.

Range: 60 – 79
Recommendation: PHONE_SCREEN
Logic: Good role match, but lacks "Strong" signals. Verify depth verbally.

Range: 40 – 59
Recommendation: ASSESSMENT
Logic: Weak signals. Require a coding assessment to prove ability.

Range: 0 – 39
Recommendation: REJECT / ALTERNATE
Logic: Role mismatch. If Fundamentals > 10, suggest "Junior/Intern" role.
